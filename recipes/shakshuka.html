Shakshuka Recipe

Shakshuka is a vibrant and hearty Middle Eastern dish featuring poached eggs nestled in a spiced tomato and bell pepper sauce. Originating from North Africa, it’s become a global breakfast and brunch favorite for its bold flavors and rustic appeal. Quick to prepare and perfect for dipping bread into, this one-pan dish is both comforting and nutritious.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 small bell pepper (any color, diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (diced) or 1 cup canned crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste (optional, for richness)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon chili flakes (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro (chopped, for garnish)
  • Crusty bread or pita (for serving)

Steps

  1. Heat the Pan: Heat olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat.
  2. Sauté the Base:
    1. Add the chopped onion and cook for 2 - 3 minutes until softened.
    2. Stir in the diced bell pepper and cook for another 4 - 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add Garlic and Spices:
    1. Add the minced garlic, paprika, cumin, and chili flakes (if using).
    2. Cook for about 1 minute, stirring frequently, until fragrant.
  4. Prepare the Tomato Sauce:
    1. Add the diced tomatoes (or canned tomatoes) and tomato paste to the skillet.
    2. Stir well to combine, then season with salt and black pepper.
    3. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for 8 - 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.
  5. Create Wells for the Eggs:
    1. Use a spoon to make two small wells in the sauce.
    2. Crack one egg into each well, being careful not to break the yolks.
  6. Cook the Eggs: Cover the skillet with a lid and cook on low heat for 5 - 7 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks remain runny (or cook longer for firmer yolks).
  7. Garnish and Serve:
    1. Remove the skillet from heat and garnish the shakshuka with fresh parsley or cilantro.
    2. Serve directly from the pan with crusty bread or pita for dipping.

Enjoy your shakshuka, a warm and satisfying dish perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even a light dinner!
