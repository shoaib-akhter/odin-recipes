Pancakes Recipe

Pancakes are a beloved breakfast classic, dating back centuries to ancient civilizations where variations were made with grains and water. Soft, fluffy, and slightly sweet, these pancakes are perfect for a cozy morning or a special brunch. Quick to whip up with pantry staples, they are versatile and can be customized with your favorite toppings.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup milk (adjust as needed for consistency)
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • Butter or oil for greasing the pan
  • Toppings (optional): Fresh fruits, syrup, butter, powdered sugar, whipped cream, or chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dry Ingredients: In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. This ensures the leavening agents are evenly distributed for fluffy pancakes.
  2. Mix the Wet Ingredients: In another bowl or a large measuring jug, combine the milk, egg, melted butter (or oil), and vanilla extract. Whisk until smooth and well combined.
  3. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients:
    1. Pour the wet mixture into the bowl with the dry ingredients.
    2. Gently whisk until the batter is smooth and there are no large lumps, but avoid overmixing (a few small lumps are okay). If the batter feels too thick, add a tablespoon or two of milk to thin it slightly.
  4. Preheat and Grease the Pan:
    1. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat.
    2. Lightly grease it with butter or oil, using a paper towel to spread evenly.
  5. Cook the Pancakes:
    1. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Use the back of a spoon to spread the batter slightly if needed.
    2. Cook until small bubbles appear on the surface and the edges look set, about 2 - 3 minutes.
    3. Flip the pancake gently with a spatula and cook the other side for 1 - 2 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Repeat and Keep Warm: Transfer cooked pancakes to a plate and cover them with a clean kitchen towel or foil to keep warm. Repeat the process with the remaining batter, greasing the pan as needed.
  7. Serve and Enjoy:
    1. Stack the warm pancakes on plates.
    2. Add your favorite toppings like syrup, fruits, or powdered sugar.

These light and fluffy pancakes are best enjoyed fresh and warm. Share them with someone special for a delightful breakfast!
