Tomato Soup Recipe

Tomato soup is a comforting and classic dish that dates back to the 19th century and has remained a favorite worldwide for its simplicity and flavor. It’s light yet hearty, perfect for cozy evenings or as an elegant starter to a meal. With just a few fresh ingredients, you can enjoy this creamy, tangy, and utterly satisfying soup in less than 30 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized ripe tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • 1 cup vegetable or chicken stock (adjust for desired consistency)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream (optional, for creaminess)
  • Fresh basil leaves (a few, for garnish)
  • Croutons or bread (optional, for serving)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Tomatoes: Wash and chop the tomatoes into small pieces. If you prefer a smoother soup, you can blanch and peel them first (place tomatoes in boiling water for 30 seconds, then transfer to ice water, and peel off the skins).
  2. Sauté the Base:
    1. Heat olive oil or butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat.
    2. Add the chopped onion and cook for 2 - 3 minutes until softened and translucent.
    3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Cook the Tomatoes:
    1. Add the chopped tomatoes to the saucepan. Stir well to combine with the onion and garlic.
    2. Cover and let the tomatoes cook down for about 5 - 7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they become soft and release their juices.
  4. Add Stock and Simmer:
    1. Pour in the vegetable or chicken stock and stir.
    2. Season with salt, black pepper, and sugar (if needed to reduce the acidity).
    3. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer uncovered for 10 minutes.
  5. Blend the Soup:
    1. Remove the saucepan from heat and allow it to cool slightly.
    2. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup directly in the pot. Alternatively, transfer the mixture to a blender and blend until smooth (work in batches if necessary).
    3. If using a blender, return the soup to the pot.
  6. Adjust Consistency and Flavor:
    1. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt or pepper if needed.
    2. For a creamier texture, stir in the heavy cream and heat gently without boiling.
  7. Serve and Garnish:
    1. Ladle the hot soup into bowls.
    2. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of cream (if desired).
    3. Serve with croutons or crusty bread on the side for dipping.

Enjoy your homemade tomato soup, a comforting bowl of deliciousness made with love!
